## The problem

We write good stuff on the atsell.io blog. Then it sits there.

The team knew the content was there. We just never had bandwidth to turn it into LinkedIn posts consistently. Every week, someone would say "we should post that". Nobody did.

So we automated it.

## What we built

A **Claude Managed Agent** that runs every Saturday at 10:30am Singapore time. It:

1. Fetches the latest post from atsell.io/blog
2. Finds the counter-intuitive angle (not just a summary)
3. Writes a **7-slide carousel script** — hook, 5 content slides, CTA
4. Generates a **ready-to-paste GPT image prompt** using our brand system (navy, gold, Playfair Display)
5. Writes a **short hook** (2–4 lines) to post alongside the carousel
6. Emails all three to jacob@atsell.io via Resend

No manual work. No prompting. Just an email in the inbox every Saturday.

## The technical setup

**Platform:** Claude Managed Agents (platform.claude.com)

**Model:** Claude Opus 4.8 — we use the most capable model because voice fidelity matters. Cheap models produce AI slop.

**Schedule:** `30 2 * * 6` (UTC) = 10:30am Asia/Singapore every Saturday

**Email:** Resend API — simpler than wiring up Gmail OAuth. Agent calls the REST endpoint directly with `curl`. Domain verified via Vercel auto-configure.

**Vault:** RESEND_API_KEY stored in CMA vault, injected as environment variable at runtime.

## The hardest part: voice

The technical setup took a few hours. Getting the voice right took longer.

The agent needed to sound like Jacob, not like a LinkedIn ghostwriter who's never left a WeWork.

What worked:
- Feeding it actual LinkedIn post examples as reference
- Explicit "never write" rules: no "In today's fast-paced world", no "Let's dive in", no "Key takeaways"
- Instructing it to find the **counter-intuitive angle** — the thing that pushes back on conventional wisdom, not just summarises the article
- Singapore-specific framing: Shopee, Lazada, TikTok Shop, the actual market we operate in

What didn't work:
- Asking it to "write in a casual tone" — too vague, still produced generic output
- Long-form posts — Jacob doesn't post walls of text; he posts carousels. The agent was originally writing a full LinkedIn post. That was wrong. The carousel IS the post.

## The output format

Three files, emailed every Saturday:

**hook.md** — 2–4 lines. Goes in the LinkedIn caption above the carousel. Short, punchy, no explanation needed.

**carousel-script.md** — 7 slides. Slide 1 is the hook that stops the scroll. Slides 2–6 are the content. Slide 7 is the CTA.

**gpt-design-prompt.md** — A complete prompt to paste into GPT Image. Includes our brand colors (navy #1e3a5f, gold #e8a838), font guidance (Playfair Display headings), and the specific slide content to render.

## What it costs

One run uses roughly 15,000–20,000 tokens (Opus 4.8). At current pricing, each Saturday run costs under $0.50.

Cheaper than a freelancer. Faster than a freelancer. Doesn't need a brief.
